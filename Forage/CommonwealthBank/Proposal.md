Subject: Proposal for Strengthening Website Hosting Security

Dear Manager,

I am writing to propose several measures to further strengthen the security of the Commonwealth Bank of Australia’s website hosting environment. As a major financial institution that manages sensitive customer information and high volumes of online transactions, maintaining strong website security is critical to protecting customer trust, preventing service disruptions, and reducing cybersecurity risks.

To further improve website hosting security, I recommend the following:

1. Reviewing and strengthening the current managed hosting environment to ensure software updates, security patches, and server monitoring are consistently maintained. Given the scale of operations and cyber threats faced by financial institutions, continuous monitoring and proactive maintenance should remain a priority to reduce vulnerabilities caused by outdated systems or configuration issues.

2. Ensuring SSL (Secure Socket Layer) certification remains fully implemented across all website services to maintain encrypted communication between customers and the website. This will continue to protect sensitive customer information, including login credentials and banking details, from interception.

3. Strengthening server-side security using a Secure File Transfer Protocol (SFTP) for secure file transfers, replacing unsecured transfer methods where applicable. In addition, reviewing and enhancing Web Application Firewall (WAF) configurations is recommended to strengthen monitoring of incoming and outgoing traffic and improve detection and blocking of malicious activity.

4. Given the increasing sophistication of cyberattacks targeting financial institutions, maintaining strong Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) protection and intrusion-prevention systems is essential to minimise disruptions to online banking services.

Further strengthening brute force attack protection through:

- stricter login controls
- bot detection
- account lockout measures, and
- multi-factor authentication (MFA)

5. Regular off-site automated backups should continue to be reviewed and tested to ensure business continuity in the event of a cyber incident, malware infection, or system failure.

Potential challenges may include increased operational costs, evolving cyber threats, and the need for ongoing system maintenance and staff cybersecurity awareness.

However, strengthening these measures will further improve website resilience, enhance customer confidence, and support Commonwealth Bank’s commitment to maintaining a secure digital banking experience.

Thank you for considering this proposal. I appreciate the opportunity to contribute recommendations that support the continued security and reliability of Commonwealth Bank of Australia’s online services.
